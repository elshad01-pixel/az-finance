### 🇬🇧 English Summary

AzFinance ERP has been audited across 7 functional phases under Azerbaijan tax and accounting standards. The system scores **26/30 points (87% compliance)**, demonstrating strong foundations in payroll tax compliance and VAT calculation accuracy.

**Payroll is the standout module**: all six employees' PIT, Social Insurance (22%+3%), Health Insurance (2%+0.5% tiered), and Unemployment (0.5%+0.5%) deductions are calculated with 100% accuracy per Azerbaijan 2026 law. The Art.102 monthly PIT deduction of 200 AZN is correctly applied only to main-workplace employees earning ≤ 2,500 AZN gross. All 10 invoices apply exactly 18% VAT with zero deviation.

**Critical business risk identified**: The Windows product (ART232323) is being sold at 10 AZN/m² against a FIFO cost of 34 AZN/m², generating a negative gross margin of −240%. This is not an ERP calculation error but represents a pricing decision that is causing direct financial loss on every unit sold.

**Procedural gaps**: GR-2026-003 remains in draft status for a PO that already has a confirmed receipt (GR-2026-004), creating a duplicate receipt risk. The June 2026 payroll run was approved in May 2026, contrary to the accrual principle. Invoice INV-1009 from a confirmed delivery remains in Draft status, causing revenue recognition lag. The Laptop product (ART-767676) has no stock receipts, creating a zero-COGS risk if it is ever sold.

The system infrastructure — FIFO costing, batch/lot tracking, procurement workflow, VAT engine, and payroll deduction logic — is technically sound and compliant with applicable Azerbaijani regulations. The identified issues are primarily procedural and pricing-related rather than systemic.
